What is Paddleboarding?

No doubt about it, standup paddle boarding (SUP) is one of the fastest-growing watersports in the UK. If you're wondering why it's become so popular and you're interested in giving it a go, or maybe you just want to find new, great locations to paddleboard, you are in the right place.

What do i need?

If you've not tried it before you may want to have a lesson or hire first before rushing out and buying a board - we have the finder tool that will help you.

However, if you are looking at buying, there are plenty of great starter kits out there and we'd recommend a basic inflatable package to get you on your way (these aren't like a lilo!) They're inexpensive, portable and easy to store. It doesn't have to be new, there's a thriving second-hand paddleboard market, which not only saves you money but is far more sustainable.

Depending when you're taking your board out you may want to consider a wetsuit or other clothing. If you're just starting up, we'd certainly recommend a 'rash' top regardless of the season, as you might be spending a fair bit of time pulling yourself back up on your board. Save on those nasty burns!

Where Can I Paddleboard?

The UK has many amazing locations, absolutely ideal for paddleboarding; beautiful lakes, over 5000 miles of scenic rivers and canals UK Canal map and of course our stunning and extensive coastline to explore.

Do remember to look into whether the location requires a waterway licence. Many managed rivers and canals in England & Wales do and you don't want to be hit with a fine. You can check the British Canoeing website to see if one is needed and if so then you can sign up and obtain a licence through them, or Canoe Wales.
